GOKU

The game consists of throwing a power with the space key and dodging the powers thrown by your enemy moving with the arrows of the keyboard, you can move around the screen of the game. You have a percentage of life just like your enemy.

PLAY

MINESMINER

The game consists in clearing all the squares of a screen that do not hide a mine. Some cells have a number, this number indicates the mines that are in all the surrounding cells. Thus, if a box has the number 3, it means that of the eight squares around (if it is not in a corner or edge) there are 3 with mines and 5 without mines. If a box is found without a number, it indicates that none of the neighboring boxes has a mine and these are discovered automatically. If a square with a mine is discovered, the game is lost. You can put a mark on the boxes that the player thinks there are mines to help discover which are nearby.

PACMAN

The objective of the game is to eat all the points in a labyrinth, taking care of four ghosts that in turn chase after him, if they reach it, they reduce Pacman as if it were a balloon that deflates, but Pacman has four points of special power to eat the ghosts, if these are reached they become floating eyes that go to the central square to dress as ghosts.

ROCK-PAPER-SCISSORS

Participate two or more players and the game develops with hands. The closed fist represents the stone, the open hand, the paper and the index and middle fingers stretched, making a vee, the scissors. The stone wins the scissors because it breaks them, the scissors win the paper because they cut it, the paper wins the stone because it envelops it. What is said is: "stone, paper or scissors", just at the end of the sentence they will show their hands and see who wins. If both children teach the same figure they tie.
